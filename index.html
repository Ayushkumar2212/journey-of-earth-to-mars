Phase 01

Earth: The Departure

Humanity's transition into a multi-planetary species starts here. Launching a spacecraft requires perfect planetary alignment.

Launch Window

Earth and Mars align optimally only once every 26 months.

Escape Velocity

The rocket must accelerate to 11.2 km/s (25,000 mph) to break free.

🌍

Phase 02

Atmospheric Ascent

Ignition. Millions of pounds of thrust fight gravity. The spacecraft endures extreme vibrations.

Max-Q

Maximum dynamic pressure point where aerodynamic stress is highest.

G-Forces

Astronauts experience up to 4Gs, making breathing difficult.

🔥

Phase 03

Trans-Mars Injection

After reaching a parking orbit, the spacecraft performs its most critical engine burn to slingshot into deep space.

Hohmann Transfer

An elliptical path using the Sun's gravity to coast to Mars.

Delta-V

A massive burst of energy is required to push out of Earth's orbit.

🛰️

Phase 04

The Deep Void

Cruising through absolute silence for 7 months. The crew must survive racing at 24,000 mph through a hostile vacuum.

Microgravity

Astronauts perform intensive resistive exercises daily.

Life Support

Recycles 98% of all water onboard into clean drinking water.

👨‍🚀

Phase 05

Orbital Insertion

As the Red Planet grows in the window, the spacecraft hits the brakes to allow Mars' gravity to capture it.

Aerocapture

Skimming the upper Martian atmosphere to use friction as a brake.

Comms Delay

Signals take 20 mins to reach Earth. All maneuvers are automated.

📡

Phase 06

7 Minutes of Terror

The Entry, Descent, and Landing sequence. The spacecraft must autonomously decelerate from 12,000 mph to zero.

Plasma Blackout

Friction heats the shield to 1,500°C, blocking all communications.

The Sky Crane

Retro-rockets halt descent, lowering rovers via nylon tethers.

☄️

Phase 07

Surface Touchdown

The dust settles. Humanity has successfully arrived. The harsh, barren landscape of the Red Planet stretches out.

Atmosphere

100 times thinner than Earth's and composed of 95% toxic CO2.

Toxic Regolith

The red dirt contains perchlorates, highly toxic to humans.

🪐

Phase 08

The First Colony

Exploration evolves into habitation. To survive long-term, astronauts deploy habitats and massive solar arrays.

ISRU Tech

Extracting frozen water to create breathable oxygen and rocket fuel.

Radiation Shielding

Habitats must be buried underground to block solar radiation.
